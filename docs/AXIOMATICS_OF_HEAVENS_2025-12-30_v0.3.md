# AXIOMATICS OF THE HEAVENS v0.3 (2025-12-30)

## 1. Ontological Axioms
1.1. The Heavens are a continuous, ordered, and differentiable manifold, denoted \( \mathcal{H} \), embedded in four-dimensional spacetime \( \mathbb{R}^4 \).
1.2. Celestial bodies are defined as persistent, locally differentiable trajectories within \( \mathcal{H} \), each with unique worldlines \( \gamma_i(t) \).
1.3. The observer is a worldline \( \gamma_O(t) \) with a privileged local frame, from which all astrometric measurements are referenced.

## 2. Epistemic Axioms
2.1. All astrological knowledge is conditional on the observer’s spacetime coordinates \( (t_O, x_O, y_O, z_O) \).
2.2. Astrological statements are formalized as mappings \( f: \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{S} \), where \( \mathcal{S} \) is a semantic space of interpretations.
2.3. The truth value of an astrological statement is a function of both celestial configuration and observer context.

## 3. Computational Axioms
3.1. The astrological chart is a finite, computable projection \( \pi: \mathcal{H} \to \mathbb{R}^n \), parameterized by observer context and epoch.
3.2. All chart calculations are reproducible, given identical initial conditions and ephemeris data.
3.3. The system supports algorithmic extension: new celestial bodies or interpretive rules may be added without loss of generality.

## 4. Semantic Axioms
4.1. Each chart element (planet, house, aspect) is mapped to a unique semantic archetype in \( \mathcal{S} \).
4.2. Interpretations are generated by compositional rules over \( \mathcal{S} \), constrained by context and prior knowledge.
4.3. The system is language-agnostic at the semantic level; natural language output is a rendering of \( \mathcal{S} \) in a target language.

## 5. Meta-Axioms
5.1. All axioms are subject to revision in light of new empirical, computational, or semantic evidence.
5.2. The system maintains full traceability of all changes to axioms and interpretive rules.
